# Press Release

**Purpose:** Official news for press and partners — credible, quotable, on the record.

**Length:** Standard PR shape: headline, dateline, lead paragraph, 2–4 body paragraphs, a quote,
and the boilerplate.

**Register:** Credible **and** electric. Factual without going flat or corporate — the news still
feels like a moment.

**Format:**
- **Headline:** the news, with energy (not a tagline, not a snooze).
- **Lead:** who/what/when/where, in the brand's voice.
- **Body:** detail and context; one strong on-brand quote.
- **Boilerplate:** use the approved text verbatim from [`../messaging.md`](../messaging.md).

**Do:** Lead with the real news. Keep facts tight. Let the quote carry the swagger.

**Don't:** Use hype clichés or banned words ([`../lexicon.md`](../lexicon.md)). Go bloodless. Bury
the news under adjectives.

**Example:** *Headline:* "The Neo Premier League Announces Its First Season — and the First Truly
Robotic Sport Begins." (Body in standard PR shape; boilerplate appended verbatim.)
