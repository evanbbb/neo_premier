# Messaging

The repeatable, approved ways the Neo Premier League describes itself. Pillars guide *what* we say;
[`voice.md`](voice.md) guides *how*. Use these as the spine for campaigns and copy.

## Message pillars

1. **A new sport is being born.** The first truly robotic sport — and we're at chapter one.
2. **Humans build, machines play.** Human ambition expressed through steel.
3. **Engineering is athletic drama.** Invention as performance; failure and rebuild as story.
4. **Real stakes, real spectacle.** A league, a season, a crown — and a stadium that roars.

Every campaign should ladder up to at least one pillar. `campaign-strategist` cites the pillar(s)
it's built on.

## Key messages (by pillar)

- **New sport:** "Not the future of an old game — the birth of a new one."
- **Humans + machines:** "Built by humans. Played by machines."
- **Engineering as drama:** "Every machine is a team's will, made of steel."
- **Stakes + spectacle:** "Wires will tear. Sparks will fly. Crowns will be claimed."

## Boilerplate

**Short (1 sentence):**
The Neo Premier League is the first truly robotic sport — where machines built by human hands
clash, score, fail, and evolve in front of roaring crowds.

**Medium (elevator):**
The Neo Premier League turns robot football into a global sport where engineering, entertainment,
and competition collide. Born from championship robotics teams, it takes elite builders out of the
lab and into the arena — a league where invention becomes athletic performance and the future is
humans building machines that play.

**Long (about):**
The Neo Premier League is a movement born from championship robotics teams: elite engineers taking
their machines out of the lab and into the arena. What began as a technical challenge is becoming a
new global spectacle — the first truly robotic sport. Here, engineering is athletic performance,
failure is part of the show, and real stakes turn a demo into a sport. Machines built by human
hands clash, score, fail, and evolve across a season, chasing a crown. The future is not humans
versus machines. It is humans building machines that play.

## Usage notes

- Lead with a pillar, not a feature.
- The boilerplate is the safe, on-brand baseline — use it verbatim where consistency matters
  (press, partner decks), and let `brand-writer` heighten it for campaign surfaces.
