# Typography

## Headline / display — British Inserat *(confirmed)*

The display face is **British Inserat** — a tall, condensed, heavy industrial grotesque. It
carries the brand's headline voice: bold, compressed, broadcast-loud.

- **Use for:** taglines, headlines, poster titles, scoreboard/broadcast graphics, jersey wordmarks.
- **Set it:** UPPERCASE, tight tracking, large. Let it dominate. Condensed weight means it can be
  huge without crowding.
- **Pairs with:** heavy contrast (white on black / black on white) and the shape language in
  [`identity.md`](identity.md).

## Body — TODO (not yet chosen)

> **The brand owner has not selected a body font yet.** Do not invent one. Until it's chosen,
> leave body-copy font unspecified in deliverables (or use a neutral system grotesque as a
> visible placeholder and flag it).
>
> When choosing, look for a clean, neutral **grotesque** that recedes under British Inserat and
> stays legible at small sizes. (Suggestions to consider, not decisions: a workhorse grotesque
> such as a neutral sans for running text.)

## Telemetry / label style — monospace *(observed system element)*

The key-art carries a layer of small **monospaced** labels and readouts: `MATCH_PROTOCOL`,
`SYNC_OK`, `// FUTURE_FORM 003`, ID strings, packet/sync counters. This is part of the HUD overlay
system, not body copy.

- **Use for:** captions, data tags, registration text, "build readout" overlays, UI chrome.
- **Set it:** small, uppercase or mixed, wide-ish tracking, often in `ash`/`graphite`.
- **Specific monospace face:** TODO — pick one and record it here.

## Hierarchy at a glance

| Level | Face | Treatment |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Display / headline | British Inserat | UPPERCASE, condensed, huge, max contrast |
| Body | _TODO_ | neutral grotesque, legible, recessive |
| Telemetry / labels | monospace (_TODO face_) | small, tracked, HUD layer |

## Don'ts

- Don't let body or telemetry compete with the British Inserat headline — it leads.
- Don't add a third "personality" face. The system is: one loud display + one quiet body + a mono
  utility layer.
- No soft, rounded, or decorative faces — they fight the engineered/industrial tone.
