# Treatment

How images are finished. The treatment turns a clean greyscale render into Neo Premier League
key-art. Three layers, used together: **glitch**, **HUD/telemetry**, and **grit**.

## 1. Glitch / datamosh *(signature)*

Horizontal pixel-tear and scan displacement pulling the subject sideways — as if the broadcast
signal is breaking under the speed of the moment.

- **Direction:** primarily horizontal streaks; can trail off one edge of the figure.
- **Intensity:** purposeful, not total — enough to convey speed/signal, never so much the athlete
  is lost. Tie the tear to motion (a kick, a header).
- **Reference:** `assets/style/key-art-hero.png`, `assets/style/key-art-header.png`.

## 2. HUD / telemetry overlay

A "build readout" layer of monospaced labels and marks (see [`identity.md`](identity.md) and
[`typography.md`](typography.md)): `MATCH_PROTOCOL`, `SYNC_OK`, `// FUTURE_FORM 003`, ID strings,
packet counts, registration ticks, vertical side text.

- Sits at the edges and corners; frames, never crowds.
- Set small, in `ash`/`graphite`, monospace.
- **Reference:** `assets/style/key-art-poster.png`.

## 3. Grit / xerox

A tactile, high-contrast, photocopied texture under it all — the garage-built, human-made hand.
Keeps everything from feeling like a clean corporate render.

- Subtle film/photocopy grain; crushed blacks; blown highlights where it serves drama.
- Never so heavy it muddies the silhouette.

## High-contrast black & white

All of the above lives in greyscale (see [`color.md`](color.md)). Push tonal contrast: deep matte
blacks against near-white grounds. Mid-greys are for backdrops and the quiet telemetry layer.

## Restraint

Treatment is meaning, not garnish. Each layer should earn its place:

- **Glitch** = speed / signal / chaos of the moment.
- **HUD** = engineering, data, the machine-readout frame.
- **Grit** = human-made, garage-built, real.

If a layer isn't doing one of those jobs, drop it. Over-treated, decorative work is an
[anti-pattern](../anti-patterns.md).
