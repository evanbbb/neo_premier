# Foundation

The bedrock of the Neo Premier League. Everything else in `brand/` answers to this file.

## One-liner

**Neo Premier League — robot football as a global sport.**

## Mission

To turn robot football into a global sport where engineering, entertainment, and competition
collide. A league for builders, players, and fans pushing machines to do the impossible.

## Vision

To create the first truly robotic sport: a competitive entertainment platform where invention
becomes athletic performance. The future is not humans versus machines — it is **humans building
machines that play.**

## Background

Born from championship robotics teams. We are a movement of elite engineers taking their machines
out of the lab and into the arena. What began as a technical challenge is becoming a new global
spectacle.

## Positioning statement

For fans of sport, gaming, robotics, and future culture, the Neo Premier League is the first
truly robotic sport — where machines built by human hands clash, score, fail, and evolve in front
of roaring crowds. Unlike sterile robotics demos or generic esports, we treat invention as
athletic performance and engineering drama as a spectator sport.

## The collision (our core idea)

Three worlds slam together and the friction is the point:

- **Engineering** — real machines, real builders, real failure and iteration.
- **Entertainment** — stadiums, rivalries, heroes, broadcast spectacle.
- **Competition** — a league, a season, a crown worth fighting for.

Hold all three at once. Drop any one and we become something we refuse to be (see
[`anti-patterns.md`](anti-patterns.md)): a lab demo, a hype shell, or a toy.

## League structure

The league fields **8 teams**: **Black Circuits**, **Rust Saints**, **Forge Union**,
**The Assembly Line**, **Graveyard Shift**, **The Stack FC**, **Null State**, and **The Latents**.
Each has its own kit — the kits share one greyscale system but each carries a distinct graphic
identity. See [`visual/kits.md`](visual/kits.md) for the team ↔ kit details.

## What we are / are not

| We are | We are not |
| --- | --- |
| A sport | A tech expo |
| Builders and competitors | Hobbyists with gadgets |
| Human-made machines | Autonomous overlords |
| Spectacle with real stakes | Novelty |
