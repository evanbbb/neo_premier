# Lexicon & Naming

The specific words the Neo Premier League uses, the words it bans, and how it names things. Used
by every text skill — especially [`name-forge`](../skills/name-forge/SKILL.md) (naming
conventions) and [`copy-reviewer`](../skills/copy-reviewer/SKILL.md) (banned words).

## Words & phrases we use

- **The sport / the league** — never "the event," "the demo," "the competition platform."
- **Titans, gladiators, machines, steel** — for the robots.
- **Builders, engineers, makers, teams** — for the humans (heroes, not staff).
- **The arena, the pitch, the stadium** — where it happens.
- **The clash, the collision, ignition, the roar** — for the action and the moment.
- **Season, crown, championship, rivalry, standings** — the competitive frame.
- **Build, rebuild, evolve, push the impossible** — the engineering arc.

## Words we ban

These pull us toward the energies in [`anti-patterns.md`](anti-patterns.md).

- **Corporate / hype filler:** synergy, solution, leverage, next-gen, cutting-edge, revolutionary,
  game-changer, disrupt, seamless, innovative (as a label), unlock, elevate, empower.
- **Flat event language:** starts soon, now live, coming soon, don't miss out, sign up today,
  tune in (as filler).
- **Cold-futurism / sterile:** the future of X, smart (as adjective), autonomous future, AI-powered.
- **Toy / novelty:** cute, fun little, gadget, toy, gizmo.
- **Versus framing of humans vs machines:** "machines take over," "rise of the machines,"
  "man vs machine." We are humans building machines that *play*.

## Naming conventions

How to name the things inside the league. `name-forge` follows these.

### Teams
Evocative, tribal, a little dangerous. One or two words. Suggest a crew of builders with an
identity. *Pattern: [adjective/noun of force or origin] — e.g. a forge, a faction, a place.*

### Robots / machines
Built things with character — part machine designation, part fighter name. May pair a model-style
tag with a fighting name. *Feel: engineered + heroic.*

### Tournaments / seasons / events
Grand, mythic, stakes-forward. Evoke a crown, a gauntlet, an ascent. Avoid generic "Championship
2026" — give it a name with blood in it.

### Arenas / venues
Cathedral-of-the-sport energy. The place where titans clash.

### Signature moves / plays
Punchy, visceral, broadcast-callable — the kind of name a commentator screams. ≤ 3 words.

### House style for names
- Prefer hard consonants and momentum over soft, abstract coinages.
- Engineering/robotics jargon and model designations are welcome — they read as engineered and
  on-brand. Just don't sound like a sterile lab demo (it's a fighter, not a "Demo Bot").
- A great name works on a jersey, a bracket, and a commentator's lips.

## Capitalization & house style

- **Neo Premier League** — full name on first use; **NPL** acceptable as a short form after.
- Sentence case for body copy; display/headline casing per [`visual/typography.md`](visual/typography.md).
- Em dashes for cinematic pauses are on-brand. Exclamation points: sparingly — the words should
  carry the energy, not the punctuation.
