# Audience

Fans of sport, gaming, robotics, science, engineering, and future culture — from hardcore
builders to casual spectators who just want to watch machines clash, score, fail, and evolve.

The audience lives on a spectrum from **maker** to **spectator**. Most work should reward both
ends at once: a layer the expert respects, and a hook the newcomer feels instantly.

## The spectrum

```
THE BUILDERS  ─────────────────────────────────────────►  THE SPECTATORS
engineers, makers          fans of the sport          casual / first-time
(go deep on the how)       (follow teams & seasons)   (here for the clash)
```

## Primary segments

### 1. The Builders
Engineers, roboticists, makers, students of the craft. They came from the lab and the workshop.

- **They want:** technical credibility, real machines, the how-it-was-made, respect for the craft.
- **They reward:** authenticity, specificity, hard-won detail.
- **They punish:** fakery, hand-waving, "magic robot" nonsense.

### 2. The Sport Fans
People who follow teams, rivalries, standings, and seasons. They came for the competition.

- **They want:** stakes, heroes, villains, narrative across a season, moments to argue about.
- **They reward:** drama with real consequences, characters they can root for.
- **They punish:** low stakes, no story, a "demo" with no league behind it.

### 3. The Spectators
Casual viewers — gaming, sci-fi, and future-culture audiences who just want to watch machines
clash. They came for the spectacle.

- **They want:** instant visceral payoff, cinematic clips, "did you see that?" moments.
- **They reward:** clarity, energy, shareability.
- **They punish:** jargon, slowness, anything that feels like homework.

## Mindsets we serve (cross-cutting)

- **The kid who took things apart** — wonder plus mischief.
- **The competitor** — needs a scoreboard and a rival.
- **The future-curious** — wants to feel they're watching history begin.
