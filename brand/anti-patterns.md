# Anti-Patterns

What the Neo Premier League avoids — in words and visuals. This is the "fail" side of every
review skill. If work drifts toward any of these, it's off-brand.

## The five energies to avoid

From the brief — the vibes that kill the brand:

1. **Sterile robotics conference** — clinical, gray, slide-deck robotics. No pulse.
2. **Toy robot novelty** — cute, harmless, gadget-store energy. No stakes.
3. **Academic demo day** — "look what we built" with no sport, no crowd, no crown.
4. **Generic esports clone** — borrowed gamer aesthetics with nothing robot or human under it.
5. **Cold corporate futurism** — glossy, bloodless, "the future of everything." No grit, no human.

## Copy anti-patterns (text)

- **Flat event language:** "starts soon," "now live," "coming soon," "don't miss out."
- **Corporate hype clichés:** "the future of sport is here," "next-gen," "revolutionary,"
  "game-changer," "experience the future."
- **Feature-led openings:** leading with specs or capabilities instead of stakes and story.
- **Human-vs-machine framing:** "rise of the machines," "man vs machine." We are humans building
  machines that *play*.
- **Over-punctuation:** leaning on exclamation points to manufacture energy the words don't earn.
- **Vague futurism:** abstractions with no concrete, physical, visceral image.

See [`lexicon.md`](lexicon.md) for the full banned-words list.

## Visual anti-patterns (image)

- **Stock-photo cleanliness** — pristine white-lab, no texture, no grit.
- **Cute / chibi robots** — toy proportions, friendly gadget vibes.
- **Generic neon esports** — purple-blue gamer gradients with no robot-sport substance.
- **Cold corporate render** — bloodless hero shot, no atmosphere, no human trace.
- **Off-model mascot** — the robot rendered without its established character (see
  [`visual/robot-character.md`](visual/robot-character.md)).
- **Decoration without meaning** — effects (glitch, grit) applied as garnish rather than to convey
  energy and the engineered/chaotic tension.

## The test

For any piece of work, ask: *does this feel like a sport with real stakes, made by humans, with
grit and spectacle?* If instead it feels like a conference, a toy, a demo, an esports template, or
a corporate future — stop and fix it.
